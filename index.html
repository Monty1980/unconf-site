An unconference is a creation of the people who attend. This free invite-only event - hosted by Square - brings together the people we know and admire, and the people we want to know better. At Intersection, you can expect a diverse mix of ~75 entrepreneurs, program creators, industry innovators, campus recruiters, students, academic leaders, technology pioneers, professors, and researchers.


We provide the theme - how academic and industry can partner and influence each other - and you decide what to talk about and what to share. Whether Intersection is your first unconference or you’re a regular, we encourage you to read Scott Berkun’s article on how to run a great unconference session and peruse the unconference article on Wikipedia.

Before You Arrive


All about you:

Help others get to know you before the event. Add your name, affiliation, email, and 3 words that describe you here. Friday evening, you’ll use the 3 words to introduce yourself to other attendees.

Leave your mark:

Intersection will only be as good as attendees make it. Be prepared to lead or participate in a session, ask questions, and show off your work. As a group on Friday, we will put together Saturday’s schedule; this will be your chance to sign up to lead a session. If you already have ideas for a session, you can add them here.

What to bring:

Please bring a laptop and adapter, and feel free to bring anything you would like to show or demo to attendees. If you need a table or space to set up your demo, please email us two weeks prior to the event.

Getting to Square

Public Transportation

We recommend taking Muni to the Van Ness Station, or BART to the Civic Center Station. Both stations are just a few minutes walk from Square.

Biking

Square offers on-site bike parking for visitors. Enter with your bike through the 1455 Market Street entrance. A front desk security guard will escort you to guest bike parking.

Driving

We recommend parking in the Civic Center Plaza Garage. It’s located at 355 McAllister Street, between Polk and Larkin, and offers hourly parking at the driver’s expense.

Arriving at Square

Security

Be sure to bring a government-issued ID. When you arrive, please check in with the front desk security team. Let them know you are here to visit Square. After checking in, proceed to the 9th floor for opening night.
